PUBLiC

My team partnered with Metro21: Smart Cities Institute, a research institute devoted to finding solutions for challenges in metro areas, to study how public art contributes to a city’s livability. PUBLiC is a mobile augmented reality product that allows users to visually express and connect with artists while learning more about existing public artworks. With direct navigation to art sites, this application lets people easily access information about artworks through the camera view. It enhances social interaction by motivating engagement in public art discussion with the community through uniquely captured experiences.

Design Process

challenge

Public art is meant to be readily accessible to everyone and increase livability within the city, however metro21 previously found that though citizens were interested in viewing public art, there was still a lack of engagement. For this project, our client wanted us to focus on the current state of public art in Pittsburgh and propose a solution that increases livability and civic engagement.

USER RESEARCH

analyzing existing data

Metro21 conducted several stakeholder interviews, field interviews, and usability tests for the Pittsburgh’s Office of Public Arts website. After reviewing that data, we individually created a customer journey map to understand how art viewers and other stakeholders interact with the city’s art.

stakeholder & customer journey map

These 2 maps helped me understand that Pittsburgh’s public art scene includes a lot of different stakeholders like artists, curators, and property owners. After creating individual maps, we decided to get together and merge our generated data to identify potential opportunities and insights that could be used.

walk-the-wall session

There is little to no interaction between artist and the art viewer

The only existing connection between the artist and the viewer is the information plaque that is placed in front of each piece.

interviewing stakeholders

Our team then moved onto interviews, where we were able to talk to (5) art viewers and a public artist. We needed a better understanding of the art viewer's process of interacting with the public art and the artists' role in this process. We then took our notes from our interviews and made an affinity diagram To organize our observations and discover common patterns.

affinity diagram

main takeaways

1. Diverse communities can be unified and grown through inclusive, interactive, multi-functional art pieces that bring people back to the artwork.

2. The level of exposure to public art is influenced by people’s daily routines. We observed that the measure of how often one views public art is dependent on where people go on a daily basis.

3. The topic of public art has a perceived certain threshold that allows people to engage with it.

why does it even matter?

Pittsburgh's public art is unengaging

This is because of existing barriers that people face when viewing art. Though it is widely known that public art has this effect — enhancing livability, this is assuming that people are engaging and that they’re talking with one another about it.

However, now we noticed that public art at its current state has a major problem: the intention of public art contradicts the reality of the actual experience. Even though there is a low threshold of entry, where there is no fee or particular time of day required to see it, people are not conversing about it.

think-aloud protocols

In order to further investigate the problem with public art’s current state in Pitt, we need to understand existing platforms that are available. So we performed think-aloud protocols and asked users to complete tasks to evaluate the usability of a public art website created by the artist, James Simon.

KEY INSIGHTS

1. Proximity

We saw that people, perhaps unknowingly, confined their experience with public art to when they were proximally close to the art piece.

2. Prior Knowledge

People thought they needed background knowledge to talk about art.

3. Privacy

People viewed art as a private, individual experience that should be unadulterated by others’ influence and interpretation.

IDEATION

storyboarding

We were ready to convert our users' feedbacks into usable prototypes. Each of us ideated several scenarios based on our insights. We held a speed dating session, where we went through all the storyboards with several participants to validate our ideas and values. We realized the idea of using an art board as a form of communication was most effective and decided to develop and combine our best solutions, while following this storyboard.
